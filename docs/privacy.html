Privacy Policy for Solah Saar

Last updated: July 9, 2026

Solah Saar ("the App") is a board game developed by Kashif Khan. This Privacy Policy explains how we handle information when you use our App.

1. Information We Collect

Solah Saar does not require you to create an account or provide any personal information such as your name, email address, or phone number.

When you use the online multiplayer feature, the App creates an anonymous, randomly generated identifier for your device via Firebase Authentication (Anonymous sign-in). This identifier is not linked to your name, email, or any other personal information, and is used only to distinguish players within a match.

2. Data Storage

Local game data — such as your settings and saved single-player/pass-and-play games — is stored only on your device and is never transmitted anywhere.

For online multiplayer, the current state of a match (board positions, whose turn it is, room codes) is stored temporarily in a Google Firebase (Firestore) database so it can be synced between the two players in that match. This data is tied only to the anonymous device identifiers described above, not to any personal identity, and is not used for any purpose beyond running the match.

3. Third-Party Services

Solah Saar uses Google Firebase (Authentication and Firestore) solely to make online multiplayer possible. Firebase's own privacy practices are described at firebase.google.com/support/privacy. The App does not integrate any advertising or analytics services, and does not sell or share data with third parties for advertising or marketing purposes.

4. Advertisements

The App does not display advertisements.

5. Children's Privacy

The App does not knowingly collect any personal information from children. The only data associated with any user, regardless of age, is the anonymous device identifier described above.

6. Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. Any changes will be posted on this page with an updated date.
